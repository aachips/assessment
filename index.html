Overview

Apple Chip Autism is not meant for diagnosis! This tool is effectively a compilation of experiences adults with intellectual developmental disabilities may run into. It has no medical or legal legitimacy as of now whatsoever. Use at your own risk. For this reason, we wish to compile metrics about the visitors who wish to utilize this information and have a vested interest in the success of the tool. See our Privacy Policy & Metrics page for more information on that.

Questionnaire structure

Assessment Measures

There are 188 questions in the measure, divided into seven sections, each representing different areas of life that can be a strength or a weakness to a disabled adult. Every one of these questions in every section, is linked to helpful terminology, information, and resources.

Calculating a score

Each of these seven sections carries a different score. There is no combined universal total. Each question will ask for a self-report of:

  1. Frequency (0-6)
  2. Severity (0-6)
  3. Hyper or Hypo- Activity (-1, 1)

For any given question, you may score between -36, to 36. A score of -36 on a measure would indicate that this happens very frequently, it is very severe and interferes with quality of life, and that this specific phenomenon represents Hypo- (low range/lack of) activity. +36 would indicate Hyper- (High range/high level of) activity, which is very frequent and very disruptive to a good quality of life.

Hypo-Activity & Hyper-Activity

Dialectical Score Modeling

An individual can exhibit in any area a mix of hyper and hypo active behaviors. Each section is scored with a negative and positive integer in the format of (negative number, positive number). One question having -36 and one having positive 36 does not cancel out to 0. Instead, each section will have a total score representing Hypo(negative) and Hyper(positive).

What a high range disordered set of behaviors may look like on any given section

I have put together this outline for a non-diagnostic Assessment tool for Adults that are on the Autism Spectrum. This meant following and moderating popular support groups for Adults on the Spectrum and keeping an index of all the Experiential Glossary for as many sub-experiences of being on the Spectrum, that has been given a name. They are experiences that span over six different Pillars of Life Experiences: Body & 'Me', Social Interactions, Executive Functioning, Independent Living, Relationships, & Play (can be sexuality-related, asexuality identities too). The measures identify if the person relates to the experience, if it is a consistent problem in their life, and rank it between Hypo- and Hyper- relations to said measure. Answers are scored on a model to the RAADS-R, however, the measures I have written are more inclusive and equitable of experiences in all of those six Pillars. We track some metrics for possible future research purposes. Based on which experiences you select as significant in your life, the tool at the end will use an algorithm to share with the subject anecdotes about other people experiencing the same things, and helpful strategies shared to help improve the quality of lives without professional interventions.

This index is modeled after several assessment tools that I would like to credit:

  • MOSAIC - Gavin de Becker's Online Threat Assessment Tool (oprah.com), which is a Domestic Violence Threat Assessment Tool featured on Oprah, and used by Secret Service to assist in the protection of Judicial Officials. The aspect of this assessment that stands out most is the interactivity of information regarding risk assessment and safety. This is a tool that saves lives.
  • Dr. Christina Maslachs MBI Maslach Burnout Inventory. Dr. Maslach has studied Workplace Burnout for several decades, been a leading researcher in the topic, and has developed an assessment tool to track workplace related burnout. The MBI utilizes a frequency measure from 0 to 6, and separates into sub-topics which accurately model the phenomenon in an easy to understand way.
  • The RAADS-R assessment is a free assessment tool for signs of Autism and ADHD. While it is not a substitute for a clinical diagnosis, it gives access to a measure which reputably has a 97% accuracy and validity rating. If you want to read about my criticisms and critiques of this assessment and how Apple Chip Autism differs, you can do so by clicking here.

Body & Me

Hyper

Very high attention given to body. Engaging in obsessive behaviors over feeding, sleeping, hygiene, grooming, bodily routine structures every day.

Hypo

Low self-awareness of body. May need to be reminded to engage in daily self-care routines. Can be at risk for self-neglect.

Interact

Hyper

Seeks out play with peers often, very strong sense of wonder. May have difficulty establishing focus on serious life matters or responsibilities. Very often seeks out social situations even at expense of sleep and self-care.

Hypo

Reclusive and reserved behavior. May like to be alone, or lack friendships and confidence to engage with peers. May be distrustful or nihilistic about motives of others. If engaged in social situations, it is generally with a closed group of people they know and trust.

Executive Functioning

Hyper

May resemble obsessive and compulsive behaviors around a home environment. Can often mask anxiety with complex repetitive focused tasks such as cleaning, writing, or other hobbies. This can look like overachiever or perfectionist behavior, which from the outside may not seem disordered, but internally there may be pervasive fear and intrusive thoughts.

Hypo

Puts off daily tasks such as cleaning, bathing, clothing, work, and commitments. This is not laziness, the person often wants to do these things desperately, but cannot break larger tasks into smaller easier tasks.

Independent Living

Hyper

Extremely independent, does not like asking others for help. Would rather do something themselves poorly than seek assistance from someone qualified. Can be very ambitious and push away intimacy with others.

Hypo

Highly reliant on others to perform day to day tasks. May need direct care provider(s) through Medicaid or family and friends.

School & Vocational

Hyper

For school age, may be a high performing student with a high grade performance. This may not be associated with genuine love of learning, but pressure to succeed academically and financially. Other areas of life may be neglected to meet superficial goals of others.

Hypo

'Under-achiever.' Difficult to establish focus or interest on tasks they are supposed to do. May do bare minimum to get by. Demand avoidance. disorganization, and sometimes oppositionally defiant, rebellious behaviors to authority.

Finances

Hyper

May be impulsive with money and spending. Can be a place where gambling and spending addictions happen. If left to own vices, can have many extraneous debts, bad credit, and may need restrictive safeguards on allotted bank account access and financial decision-making.

Hypo

This may resemble 'pack-rat' behavior. Not wanting to let go of money. Extreme poverty mindsets around spending and saving. May forget to or neglect paying bills. Might have financial illiteracy and consult someone they trust before making a spending decision.

Both

Vulnerable to exploitation and addiction. May benefit from financial education programs, appointing a trustee or conservator, and having specialized financial accounts.
